How Age Calculation Works

Age calculation sounds simple — subtract birth year from current year — but accurate age requires accounting for whether the current year's birthday has occurred yet. If today is March 15 and your birthday is April 1, you haven't turned your next age yet even though the year subtraction suggests you have.

This calculator computes the exact age: years complete, then remaining months, then remaining days. It also handles edge cases like February 29 birthdays (leap day birthdays) and gives your total age in days, hours, and minutes for the curious.

Age Milestones Worth Knowing

Various age thresholds carry legal or cultural significance in the United States and internationally:

  • 13 — COPPA age threshold for online services; teenager age begins
  • 16 — Driving age in most US states
  • 18 — Legal adult in most countries; US voting age; military service
  • 21 — Legal drinking age in the United States
  • 25 — Car rental without surcharge; prefrontal cortex fully developed
  • 59½ — IRA/401(k) withdrawals allowed without penalty
  • 62 — Earliest Social Security retirement benefits
  • 65 — Medicare eligibility in the US
  • 67 — Full Social Security retirement age for those born after 1960

How do I calculate my exact age from my birth date? ›

To calculate your exact age: (1) Start with the difference in calendar years. (2) Check if your birthday this year has passed — if not, subtract 1 from the years. (3) Count remaining months since your last birthday. (4) Count remaining days within the current month. Example: Born June 15, 1990; today is March 10, 2024. Full years: 2024 − 1990 = 34, minus 1 because June hasn't come yet = 33. Months since last birthday (June 15): July, Aug, Sep, Oct, Nov, Dec, Jan, Feb = 8 months, plus partial March = 8 months 24 days. Age: 33 years, 8 months, 23 days.

What day of the week was I born on? ›

Use the Zeller's Congruence or Doomsday algorithm to calculate the day of the week for any historical date — or simply enter your birth date above and the calculator shows it instantly. Fun facts: more babies are born on weekdays (especially Tuesday and Wednesday) than weekends because many planned births and C-sections are scheduled Monday–Friday. Saturday and Sunday have the lowest birth counts. The most common birthday in the US is September 9 — nine months after December's holiday season.

What if my birthday is February 29 (leap day)? ›

Leap day birthdays (February 29) occur only in leap years — roughly every 4 years (with exceptions for century years). People born on Feb 29 are called "leaplings" or "leap year babies." In non-leap years, most leaplings celebrate on either February 28 or March 1 depending on personal preference and local law. The UK and Hong Kong legally recognize March 1 as the birthday for non-leap years; the US has no federal standard. This calculator handles Feb 29 birthdays correctly, displaying February 28 as the anniversary in non-leap years.

How do I calculate the age difference between two people? ›

Calculate each person's age using their birth date and today's date, then subtract. Or more precisely: (1) Subtract the earlier birth year from the later birth year. (2) Check if the younger person's birthday has passed relative to the older person's — adjust by 1 if needed. For exact calculation to the day, use our date calculator to find the number of days between the two birth dates and divide by 365.25. Example: Born Oct 5, 1985 and April 12, 1992 — the difference is approximately 6 years, 6 months, 7 days.

How old am I in days, hours, and minutes? ›

Calculate your total days old by multiplying your full years by 365 and adding the remaining days (accounting for leap years). Then multiply by 24 for hours and 1,440 for minutes. Approximately: 20 years = 7,305 days = 175,320 hours. 30 years = 10,958 days = 263,000 hours. 40 years = 14,610 days = 350,640 hours. 50 years = 18,263 days = 438,315 hours.
